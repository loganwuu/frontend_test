Employee Conference Information

Will I need a Passport or Visa?

Passports and visas are the responsibility of the traveler. Please direct any questions regarding visas to Polycom’s preferred visas and passports vendor, details of which can be found on the travel page of Planet Polycom.

Passports are required to enter Canada. If you do not have a passport at this time, the process to apply for and obtain a passport can take up to six weeks for U.S. citizens and other countries may vary. So, please plan ahead. If you have a current passport, please make sure your passport doesn't expire within six months of your Vancouver departure date.

Please click here for a list of countries that require a visa for entrance into Canada. Note that all visa requests should be submitted by within one week of being invited to attend TEAM Polycom in order to guarantee your best chance of approval. We had employees who were not able to attend last year because there was not time to resubmit a denied request. Please use this template if you need an invitation letter for your visa. Contact Polycom’s Global Meeting Manager, Amy Drotar, for additional assistance—she can direct you to our lawyer who specializes in this topic if required.

What Expenses Should I Anticipate Incurring During the Event?

Refer to the Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy for reminders on expenses that are reimbursable. Note that personal expenses such as movies, minibar, room service and upgrades are considered personal and are not reimbursable. Wireless Internet access is included in the hotel room rate and should not be expensed.

Accounting has been notified to expect a large number of TEAM Polycom expense reports that will include: airfare; ground transportation between your home and the airport; airport parking, if applicable; meals during travel and any meals other than group meals as outlined below. These costs, along with any passport or visa costs, must be charged to your corporate credit card as these costs are not covered by TEAM Polycom master billing. Accounting has also been advised that:

  • Group meals will be provided beginning with dinner on Tuesday, February 11 and end with lunch on Thursday, February 13, 2014. No meal expenses submitted during that time will be approved for payment.
  • Polycom is hosting a Special Events After Party for our Partners and all Channel Account Managers are required to attend. Please note: There will be no allowances for dinner expenses on this evening.
  • A regular shuttle bus service will operate on arrival and departure dates between the hotel and the airport, therefore no rental cars or taxi charges should be submitted. Approval will only be provided under unusual circumstances which should be noted on your expenses.

Tips for Using Cell Phones in Canada

All employees attending TEAM Polycom in February 2014 in Vancouver Canada will need additional international voice, data and text features added to their cell phone plans.

  • There will be free WiFi coverage at the hotel and at the convention center. In order to avoid major data overages, please connect to the free WiFi whenever possible. Do not download or send large email attachments from your email while using your cell phone data plan. This uses a large amount of data causing excessive overages.
  • Employees on the US corporate paid plans will be updated automatically. In order to ensure all employees attending TEAM Polycom have the necessary international cell phone coverage while at the event, the Cell Phone administrators will be adding international coverage for voice, data, and text to all TEAM Polycom attendees who are currently on the US Corporate AT&T or Verizon mobile phone accounts. Additional details will be sent out prior to the event.
  • Employees who are not on the plan, are outside the US, or submit expenses for cell phone charges or other devices will need to contact their carriers and arrange Canada / International coverage themselves. Please contact your carrier and add the necessary international features to your mobile phone account to ensure you are covered while in Canada. If international features are not added, your mobile phone bill will not be eligible for reimbursement for your time at TEAM Polycom.
  • If you have any questions/concerns, please contact the Polycom Cell Phone Administration at 408-586-4500 or via email at CellPhoneAdministration@polycom.com

What does Polycom provide for International Safety and Security during travel?

  • Employees should visit the International SOS (ISOS) web site and obtain a laminated ISOS card or upload the ISOS application to their cellphone with the necessary contact information, in advance of your trip. Polycom’s member number is #11BYCA000075. The program provides the tools you need to stay safe and secure while traveling internationally, including emergency medical, security, and logistical expertise. The traveler will be able select the destination country or city and review relevant content on safety, security, medical resources, culture, etc. It is critical for Polycom employees to become familiar with the customs and laws of each country they visit when traveling internationally.
  • ISOS works closely with ACE Global Medical and Business Travel Accident Insurance who covers our employees while travelling on business, so one call is all you need to make.

When and How Do I Book Flights?

This year we will be implementing a new travel procedure for TEAM Polycom. All travel must be booked through the TEAM Polycom Group Travel Desk. These agents are dedicated to our Polycom employees attending TEAM Polycom from all over the world. This will cut down on wait times, provide consistent implementation of the travel policy and allow us to track our attendees for safety and security purposes.

In North America, you will be provided a link to book your flights on a special GetThere link that works with our TEAM Polycom Group Travel Desk. It will look very similar to the way you typically book flights using Polycom’s online booking tool. Additional information and training sessions on how to book your flights will be sent out prior to the deadline of January 7, 2014.

Outside of North America, you will see a place to request your travel during the registration process. A travel agent from the TEAM Polycom Group Travel Desk will contact you to confirm your flight options and book your flights for you.

Booking Deadline

All flights must be booked according to the Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy. Flights booked outside the TEAM Polycom Group Travel Desk are not reimbursable. The lowest logical fare will be booked according to Polycom Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy. Due to high volume, personal requests for specific airlines cannot be taken into consideration. There will be no exceptions and no escalation process.

Additional information and training sessions on how to book your flights based on your region will be sent out prior to the deadline of January 7, 2014.

Ticketing Deadline

The TEAM Polycom ticketing deadline for ALL flights will be 5:00 p.m. US Pacific Time on Tuesday, January 7, 2014. Any flights not booked by this date require special leadership approval as defined in the approval section.

Flight Changes

Please be careful when booking flights to avoid unnecessary expenses. Once a ticket is issued, no changes are permitted without leadership approval as defined in the approval section in order to avoid expensive airline change fees. Consider the entire cost when booking flights. Note that changing flights directly through the airline (even if you pay for your own change fee) is not permitted because Polycom loses control of the ticket and we can no longer assist you, or locate you in case of an emergency. Should the employee make a change directly with the airline, they will then be responsible for the entire cost of the ticket.

Please check the dates and connection times of your flights prior to booking or approving flights to avoid flight change costs.

Flight Upgrades

The Global Travel Policy allows for frequent flyer membership upgrades, only if there is no additional cost to the company. These upgrades should be booked directly with the airline only after being ticketed by Polycom Travel. Most airlines require booking/ticketing at a higher fare in order to have a chance to get upgraded which is not guaranteed. Make sure you review your options before your ticket is approved and issued, no changes will be permitted after the ticketing. Employees are not allowed to book themselves at a higher fare in order to use frequent flyer upgrade privileges when a lower fare exists. You may add upgrade seats, but you may not change the ticket because Polycom loses control of the ticket and we can no longer assist you, or locate you in case of an emergency. Should the employee make a change directly with the airline, they will then be responsible for the entire cost of the ticket.

Trip Extensions for Personal Reasons

Any extension of your visit to TEAM Polycom for personal reasons beyond the dates listed above must be disclosed upfront and pre-approved prior to ticketing, even if the cost is the same or you are making your own hotel reservations. In order to obtain approval, you will need to follow these steps:

  1. Have approved PTO, if the extension includes a work day
  2. Employees must ask a travel agent from the TEAM Polycom Group Travel Desk to provide a comparison of the flight costs between the normal lowest logical fare over the original dates and the flight with the personal portion included.
  3. Employees should then send an email request to Alla Neys in Corporate Travel for personal travel approval, with the comparison attached.
  4. Alla will provide you and the travel agency with the approval and payment process for your region prior to ticketing. If approval is not obtained prior to ticketing, the employee will be responsible for the cost of the airline ticket.
  5. Any additional flight costs for adding a personal extension in Vancouver will be collected via credit card when the ticket is issued. The ticket is not changeable or refundable once ticketed. Any additional expenses for the personal portion of the trip, including transportation, meals and hotel stays, will be paid by the employee based on policies for their region.

  6. A Saturday night stay, in some instances (mostly on international trips), can produce significant savings. Polycom will reimburse the cost of the room, tax, and meals if the total does not exceed 50% of the amount saved.

    • Personal activities (tours, sightseeing) are not reimbursable
    • The traveler is to stay at the hotel within the same price range and room type
    • Proper documentation and pre-approval from Corporate Travel must be attached to the expense report

Note that changing flights directly through the airline (even if you pay for your own change fee) is not permitted because Polycom loses control of the ticket and we can no longer assist you, or locate you in case of an emergency. Should the employee make a change directly with the airline, they will then be responsible for the entire cost of the ticket.

Although extensions in Vancouver are permitted, side trips to other cities are not permitted along the trip.
